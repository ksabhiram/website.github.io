Carrier Concentration Optimisation in Pb1−xSnxTe via Aliovalent Doping: A Combined Transport and Defect Study
Kurapati S. Abhiram, Ravi Kumar, Sanjay Bhatt, et al.
Journal of Materials Chemistry A, 2024, 12, 8452–8466
We present a systematic study of aliovalent doping in the PbSnTe alloy system, demonstrating that simultaneous substitution at both cation sites enables fine-grained tuning of the Fermi level position. Temperature-dependent Hall measurements and DFT supercell calculations are combined to construct a unified defect-transport model, achieving a peak zT of 1.8 at 850 K.

Kubelka-Munk Analysis of Diffuse Reflectance Spectra in Narrow-Gap Thermoelectrics: Justification via the Klein Criterion
Kurapati S. Abhiram, Ananya Sharma
Submitted to Physical Review Materials · 2025
Diffuse reflectance spectroscopy using an integrating sphere is widely employed to extract optical band gaps, yet the validity conditions for the Kubelka-Munk transformation are rarely stated. We derive a practical criterion following Klein et al. (2023) and benchmark it against four narrow-gap chalcogenide systems, finding significant deviation from linearity when the scattering-to-absorption ratio falls below threshold.

Point Defect Phonon Scattering in GeTe-Based Alloys: The Abeles Parameter and Its Experimental Verification
Kurapati S. Abhiram, P. Babu, V. Subramanian, et al.
Applied Physics Letters, 123, 182104 (2023)
The Abeles scattering parameter γ is extracted experimentally from thermal conductivity measurements across a composition-graded series of Ge₁₋ₓSnₓTe samples. We identify a clear crossover between mass-disorder and strain-field dominated regimes and propose an extended model that reconciles discrepancies in previously reported lattice thermal conductivities.

● Preprint · 2025
Raman Spectroscopy as a Non-Invasive Probe of Carrier Density in Degenerate Thermoelectric Semiconductors
Kurapati S. Abhiram, Leela Mohan, D. Rao
arXiv:2503.14872 [cond-mat.mtrl-sci] · 2025
We demonstrate that the frequency and lineshape of the longitudinal-optic phonon–plasmon coupled mode in degenerate PbTe can be used to infer carrier concentration non-invasively. A self-consistent dielectric model incorporating free-carrier screening is fitted to temperature-dependent Raman spectra, recovering carrier densities within 15% of Hall-effect values across four decades of concentration.
